Barbara Siegel Carlson is the author of four books of poetry, most recently [Current](https://lilypoetryreview.blog/lily-poetry-review-press/) published by Lily Poetry Review Books, 2026. Her previous books are [What Drifted Here](https://www.amazon.com/What-Drifted-Barbara-Siegel-Carlson/dp/1625494246), [Once in Every Language](https://kelsaybooks.com/products/once-in-every-language), and [Fire Road](https://www.amazon.com/Fire-Road-Barbara-Siegel-Carlson/dp/1935716247/ref=sr_1_8?dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.Zr1INmzBqAyBDZ49DM5nyf3HGW8NiP__iiLGJ2q1Qq2xLdegSt9_3eiXAXGLOnfmcHhV9fZi0fJ5hHuI86uKm6jqR1YaKirU7LRfK88tFUA.HrGPJZwVe1OA-rE_9tL1HIIwmWuxrhAkQ0dxszu--VY&dib_tag=se&qid=1768837170&refinements=p_27%3ABarbara+Siegel+Carlson&s=books&sr=1-8). She is the co-translator (with Ana Jelnikar) of [Look Back, Look Ahead, Selected Poems of Sreƒçko Kosovel](https://www.amazon.com/Look-Back-Ahead-Srecko-Kosovel/dp/1933254548). Her poetry and translations have appeared in On the Seawall, Verse Daily, Mid-American Review, Salamander, 2River, The Poetry Porch and other journals. Her poems have been nominated for Best of the Net and the Pushcart Prize. Carlson is a Poetry in Translation Editor of [Solstice: A Magazine of Diverse Voices](https://solsticelitmag.org/issue/winter-2025/). She lives in Carver, Massachusetts.
